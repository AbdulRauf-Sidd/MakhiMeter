Image Colorization



The images of fruit fly is usually in grayscale and grayscale images can limit the ability to visually distinguish and analyze the various physical characteristics of Drosophila melanogaster, such as eye color, body color, and gender-specific traits. The pigmentation of Drosophila melanogaster is determined by pigments produced by specialized cells called ommochrome cells. These pigments are synthesized through a series of enzymatic reactions from amino acids, regulated by pigmentation pathway genes. Mutations in these genes can alter eye color or lead to a loss of pigmentation. Colorizing these grayscale images can provide researchers with more informative and visually appealing representations, aiding in the detailed phenotypic analysis of the fruit flies
