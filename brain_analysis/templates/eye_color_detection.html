Eye Color Detection



The eye color of Fruit Fly is a key phenotypic trait used by researchers to study genetic variations and mutations. The eye color of fruit flies is controlled by several pigmentation pathways, primarily the pteridine and ommochrome pathways. These pathways involve a complex series of enzymatic reactions that synthesize pigments from precursors like tryptophan and guanine. In wild-type fruit fly, these pigments combine to produce the characteristic brick-red eye color. However, mutations in genes that control these pathways can lead to a variety of eye colors, such as white, brown, sepia, or vermilion. Analyzing these color variations can be crucial for genetic research and understanding the effects of different mutations on eye pigmentation.
